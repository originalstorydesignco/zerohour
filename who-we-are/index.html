Our Story

The Zero Hour movement started with our founder, 15-year-old Jamie Margolin, who, for the past few years, had been working tirelessly to get her state of Washington to pass common sense climate change laws in order to protect her generation’s rights to a livable future – before it was too late. Jamie has always been an outspoken advocate for centering youth in the climate justice movement and was frustrated by the inaction of our elected leaders in government.

No politicians listened to her, and by the summer of 2017 she realized that a massive, national and international movement of youth was needed in order to make leaders pay attention.

She came up with a vision of youth mass mobilization to bring young people to the center of the conversation on climate action and hold their leaders accountable.

Jamie reached out to young people she knew about her vision, who rallied around it and then reached out to their friends.

By the end of the summer, their team was growing across the country, from coast to coast. Late night calls, sketches, emails and research took over these young people’s lives as they worked to make their dream a reality. Now, there is a fully fledged team determined to lift the voices of youth on climate.

The Zero Hour team has become more than just an organization – we’ve become a family.
